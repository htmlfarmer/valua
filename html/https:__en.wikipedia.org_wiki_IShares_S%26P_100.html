iShares S&P 100

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

iShares S&P 100 Index Fund is an exchange-traded fund of US stocks. The fund tracks the S&P 100 index.

Its ticker is OEF.

Holdings[edit]

Top ten holdings:

  • Apple Inc. (AAPL)
  • Exxon Mobil Corp. (XOM)
  • International Business Machines (IBM)
  • AT&T Inc. (T)
  • Microsoft Corp. (MSFT)
  • General Electric Co. (GE)
  • Citigroup Inc. (C)
  • Chevron Corp. (CVX)
  • Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)
  • Coca-Cola Co. (KO)
  • Pfizer Inc. (PFE)

(as of June 2012)

Competitors[edit]

iShares S&P 100 mainly competes with S&P 500 funds, including SPDR S&P 500 (NYSE Arca: SPY), iShares Core S&P 500 (NYSE Arca: IVV), and Vanguard S&P 500 ETF (NYSE Arca: VOO). Those funds are not mega; one fund that is more similar is Guggenheim Russell Top 50 Mega Cap (NYSE Arca: IWL).[1][2][3]
